# Meridian Support Launch Pack

This pack exists for one reason:

**help the founder talk about support publicly without blurring support, pilot, and customer revenue.**

Use this only while the system state remains:
- runtime healthy
- treasury still blocked
- honest customer path = founder-led manual pilot
- support path = backing the build, not buying delivery

If that state changes, update this pack.

If the story ever starts drifting, re-read:
- [Meridian Doctrine](MERIDIAN_DOCTRINE.md)
- [Outreach Pack](OUTREACH_PACK.md)

---

## The Core Rule

Support messaging should feel:
- direct
- human
- slightly vulnerable
- specific
- non-performative

Support messaging should not feel:
- like a pitch deck
- like a donation beg
- like fake traction theater
- like a hidden product checkout

Say what Meridian is.
Say what stage it is in.
Say what support means.
Do not say more than that.

## What To Say In Plain Language

The clean public version is:

> I’m building Meridian.  
> The open part is a constitutional kernel for running AI agents with rules, budgets, audit trails, and sanctions.  
> The first working vertical on top is competitor intelligence.  
> The system is real, but I’m keeping the customer path honest: manual pilot now, automated delivery later when treasury policy allows it.  
> If you want to support the build itself, there’s now a separate support path.

That is enough.

## Platform Notes

### Threads

Best for:
- a more personal tone
- low-friction curiosity
- “I’m building this” energy

Keep it:
- reflective
- direct
- not too technical in the first paragraph

### X

Best for:
- compressed thesis
- sharper framing
- links

Keep it:
- tighter
- a little more opinionated
- still honest

### Reddit / VOZ

Best for:
- longer explanation
- inviting critique
- technical + philosophical framing

Keep it:
- grounded
- not self-important
- explicitly open to feedback

### Instagram / casual social

Best for:
- short founder note
- build-in-public energy

Keep it:
- simple
- human
- not overloaded with theory

## The One Thing To Avoid

Do not write a giant “vision dump” post that tries to explain:
- the entire economy
- the entire philosophy
- the entire contributor future
- the entire company structure

That makes people admire the ambition and ignore the actual entry points.

Keep the post anchored on:
- what exists
- what stage it is in
- what link to click next

---

## Final Check Before Posting

If your draft implies any of these, rewrite it:

- support = buying the service
- support = customer traction
- the hosted path is already polished self-serve
- contributors are already being paid from treasury at scale
- Meridian is already the full future it only intends to become later

If your draft feels a little smaller than your dream, that is usually a good sign.
