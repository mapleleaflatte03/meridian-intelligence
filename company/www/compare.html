Pick by problem, not by hype

Need governed paid work

Pick Meridian when the job must survive approval, budget pressure, evidence checks, and sanctions without lying about the boundary.

Need broad assistant UX

Pick OpenClaw or OpenHands when the conversation is really about surface area, companion feel, or coding-platform breadth.

Need orchestration infrastructure

Pick LangGraph, CrewAI, or OpenFang when the buyer is mostly choosing runtime primitives, framework power, or one-box execution density.

Need persistent local agency

Pick TEMM1E or Goose when the story is memory, local operator feel, or direct engineering-task ergonomics more than institutional control.

Meridian still has to earn three things

01Memory and self-extension. Meridian has real memory and governed synthesis now, but not yet the same density or operator feel as the best field systems.
02Packaging and onboarding. It still trails the field on install smoothness, first-run polish, and instant recognizability.
03Mindshare. The thesis is sharper than the current public attention. That is a distribution gap, not a category correction.
What Meridian should copy is quality, not hype. It should copy packaging discipline, onboarding speed, and memory ergonomics from the strongest systems. It should not fake breadth, self-serve maturity, or delivery claims just to sound larger than it is.
