# Meridian Doctrine

This document exists to remove one recurring confusion:

**Meridian is not "an open-source repo where anyone shows up and money auto-splits equally."**

That is not the current reality, and it is not a safe promise.

What Meridian is trying to become is more serious:

**a founder-led company building an open constitutional kernel, with a long-term goal of governed contributor payouts backed by real treasury rules.**

## Meridian, Simply

Today, Meridian has three distinct layers:

1. **Meridian**  
   The company and live product effort.

2. **Meridian Constitutional Kernel**  
   The open-source artifact. Public, inspectable, and reusable.

3. **Competitive intelligence**  
   The first managed vertical used to prove the system creates real value.

These layers support each other, but they are not the same thing.

---

## What Meridian Is

Meridian is:

- a founder-led company
- building governed AI operations instead of ungoverned agent chaos
- using an open-source kernel as the constitutional core
- proving the model through a first vertical before pretending the whole system is mature

Right now, the live offer is:

- a founder-led manual pilot
- built on real workflow logic and real outputs
- with automated delivery intentionally paused until treasury policy is healthy enough to fund it honestly

---

## What Meridian Is Not

Meridian is not:

- a DAO
- an equal-split collective
- a promise that every open-source contributor automatically gets paid
- a fully production-scale hosted service for any customer, today
- a fake "support us" wrapper hiding an unfinished paid product

If we say otherwise, trust breaks.

---

## The Four Money Paths

There are four different money paths in this project. They must stay separate.

### 1. Support / Sponsorship

Purpose:
- support the work
- support infra
- support founder time
- support open-source progress

What it is not:
- not a purchase order
- not a service contract
- not proof of customer traction
- not a guaranteed payout claim for all contributors

### 2. Manual Pilot

Purpose:
- test whether the output is useful
- learn whether a team actually wants this
- keep the promise small enough to honor honestly

What it is not:
- not broad self-serve automation
- not enterprise onboarding
- not proof that the full hosted system is production-ready

### 3. Customer Revenue

Customer revenue begins only when a team is actually paying for useful output or continuation.

This is different from:
- owner capital
- donations
- sponsorship

Those must never be mixed in storytelling or accounting.

### 4. Contributor Payouts

This is the long-term direction, not the current blanket promise.

The intended future model is:
- real treasury
- real contribution evidence
- governed payout proposals
- review / approval
- constitutional dispute handling if needed

That is very different from:
- "everyone gets an equal split because they touched the repo"

Equal split sounds generous. In practice it usually becomes unfair, gameable, or impossible to sustain.

The durable version is:

**people who contribute real value can be rewarded from treasury under explicit rules.**

## Where The Founder Stands

The founder is not outside the system.

The founder is:

- the builder
- the operator
- the first seller
- the first accountable contributor

That means the founder can benefit from the company and treasury.

But the founder should not rely on vague mythology like:
- "I opened the repo, therefore the system should magically pay everyone including me"

The healthier logic is:

- build something real
- prove value
- attract real support or customers
- define fair rules
- then pay contributors, including the founder, through those rules

---

## The Correct Sequence

The wrong sequence is:

1. open-source the repo
2. wait for the world to show up
3. auto-split money

The correct sequence is:

1. build something real
2. earn trust
3. attract the right early users and contributors
4. separate support, pilot, and customer paths honestly
5. accumulate real treasury
6. introduce governed payouts only when the rules and evidence are strong enough

---

## The Current Truth

As of now:

- the kernel is public
- the live system is real
- the runtime is healthy
- the automated pipeline is treasury-gated by policy
- the honest offer is a founder-led manual pilot
- contributor economy is still a direction, not a mature reality

That is not failure.

That is simply the stage Meridian is in.

---

## The Near-Term Model

In the near term, Meridian should speak plainly:

- **Support the work** if you want to help it continue.
- **Try a manual pilot** if you want to test the output.
- **Become a customer** only when the value is proven and the delivery promise is real.
- **Contribute to the kernel** if you believe in the direction, knowing payouts require real treasury and explicit rules.

Those four offers can coexist.

They just must not be blurred together.

---

## The Long-Term Ambition

The long-term ambition is still large:

**a governed contributor economy where real digital labor can be organized, audited, and paid fairly.**

But the way to reach that ambition is not fantasy.

It is:
- smaller promises
- honest accounting
- explicit rules
- real evidence
- real treasury

---

## Decision Rule

If there is ever confusion about what Meridian should say publicly, use this rule:

**prefer the narrower claim that can be honored today over the bigger claim that might only be true later.**

That rule protects:
- trust
- the founder
- contributors
- the company
- the project itself
